Mountain

A mountain is a landform that rises prominently above its surroundings, typically with steep slopes and a peak or summit. Mountains are generally taller and steeper than hills. They are formed by tectonic forces or volcanic activity, and can be found individually or in ranges.

Kalsubai is the highest peak in Maharashtra, India, standing at 1,646 meters (5,400 feet). It's located in the Sahyadri mountain range, within the Ahmednagar district, and is often referred to as the "Everest of Maharashtra" due to its height. The peak is a popular trekking destination, especially known for its sunrise views.

Here's a more detailed look at Kalsubai: Location: The peak is situated in the Western Ghats, specifically in the Ahmednagar district of Maharashtra.

Height: It reaches a height of 1,646 meters (5,400 feet). Popularity: Kalsubai is a well-known trekking spot, attracting both experienced trekkers and beginners. Sunrise Views: The peak offers stunning views, particularly at sunrise, which is a major draw for many trekkers.

Trekking Experience: The trek involves navigating through various terrains, including forests, grasslands, and waterfalls. There are also iron ladders to help climbers ascend the steeper sections, and the trail is divided into two sections, from Bari village to the ladder section and from the ladder section to the summit, according to Indiahikes. Base Village: The trek typically starts from Bari village.
