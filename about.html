About us

Phone

A phone, in its most basic sense, is a device used for voice communication over a distance, originally via a physical wire connection (landline) and later wirelessly through cellular networks. Modern phones, particularly smartphones, offer a wide array of functionalities beyond basic calls, including internet access, text messaging, multimedia capabilities, and more. They have evolved from simple communication tools to powerful, portable computers that are integral to modern life.

Vision

The vision for phones, both landlines and mobile, has always been about connecting people. The history of the telephone involves early inventions like the string telephone and the Reis telephone, culminating in Alexander Graham Bell's first practical telephone in 1876.

Mission

To provide a portable and user-friendly device that facilitates real-time verbal communication, evolving over time to encompass diverse features and functions beyond voice calls.

History

Motorola was the first company to produce a handheld mobile phone. On 3 April 1973, Martin Cooper, a Motorola researcher and executive, made the first mobile telephone call from handheld subscriber equipment, placing a call to Dr. Joel S. Engel of Bell Labs, his rival.
